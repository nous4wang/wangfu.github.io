Profile

Fu Wang is a Senior Engineer at the CMA Earth System Modeling and Prediction Centre (CEMC), China Meteorological Administration (CMA), and a World Meteorological Organization (WMO) expert. In March 2024, he was a Visiting Scholar at the Faculty of Science and Technology (FST), University of Macau, conducting collaborative research on satellite data assimilation and machine learning applications in numerical weather prediction.

From 2015 to 2021, he worked at the National Satellite Meteorological Center (NSMC) of CMA, focusing on aerosol–cloud interactions, satellite-based aerosol and cloud monitoring, water cycle processes, and the development of operational retrieval algorithms for multispectral meteorological satellite observations. Since September 2021, he has been with CEMC, where his research centers on the assimilation of satellite-derived wind and precipitation observations into operational numerical weather prediction (NWP) systems, along with the integration of machine learning techniques into forecasting frameworks.

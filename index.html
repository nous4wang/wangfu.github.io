Profile

Fu Wang, Ph.D., is a Senior Engineer at the CMA Earth System Modeling and Prediction Centre (CEMC), China Meteorological Administration, and a World Meteorological Organization (WMO) expert. His research focuses on numerical weather prediction (NWP), satellite data assimilation, remote-sensing retrieval algorithms, and machine-learning applications in atmospheric sciences. He has published more than 50 peer-reviewed journal papers and holds over 10 authorized Chinese invention patents. His work integrates satellite observations and AI techniques to improve operational weather forecasting systems. He received the National Young Scientist Team Award in 2019 and the CMA Outstanding Numerical Weather Prediction Report Award in 2024, and serves as a reviewer for several leading journals including IEEE TGRS, JSTARS, GRSL, International Journal of Digital Earth, GIScience & Remote Sensing,Atmospheric Environment, and Atmospheric Research.

Research Interests

  • Numerical Weather Prediction (NWP) and satellite data assimilation (wind fields, precipitation)
  • Remote-sensing retrieval algorithms for multispectral meteorological satellite observations
  • Machine learning for retrieval, assimilation enhancement, and operational forecasting
  • Atmospheric remote sensing and related Earth system applications
